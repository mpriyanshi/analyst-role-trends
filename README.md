# Evolving Role of Analysts in IT Services: A Data and Market Driven Study

## Overview
This project analyzes how analyst roles are evolving within the IT services sector by examining trends in role distribution, compensation, and experience levels. The objective is to derive data-driven insights that reflect changing workforce expectations and the growing importance of analytics in business decision-making.

---
## Dataset
Source: Public job market dataset (600+ records)  
Key attributes used:
  - Job Title  
  - Salary (USD)  
  - Experience Level  
  - Company Location  
  - Remote Work Ratio  

---

## Methodology
 Data cleaning and preprocessing using Python (Pandas)  
 Standardization of job titles for consistency  
Role classification into:
  - Analyst  
  - Engineer  
  - Scientist  
  - Other  
 Comparative analysis across:
  - Role distribution  
  - Salary (mean and median)  
  - Experience level distribution  
 Regional segmentation to analyze India-specific trends  

---

## Key Insights

**Role Distribution:**  
  Engineering (~38%) and Data Science (~32%) roles dominate the dataset, while Analyst roles (~20%) form a distinct segment focused on business insights  

  **Compensation Trends:**  
Analyst roles (~$93K average) offer lower compensation compared to Engineering (~$110K) and Data Science (~$115K), highlighting a premium on specialized technical roles  

  **Experience Patterns:**  
  ~80% of analyst roles are concentrated at mid and senior levels, indicating increasing expectations for experience and domain knowledge  

 **Regional Insights (India):**  
  Role distribution in India shows a relatively balanced mix across categories, reflecting localized hiring patterns in outsourcing-driven IT services
